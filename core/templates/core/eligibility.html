Donor Eligibility Quiz

Check your current clinical status before registering as an active volunteer.

Step 1 of 6

1. Are you between 18 and 65 years old?

The safe minimum age threshold set by medical authorities is 18 years, with a maximum of 65 years for voluntary blood donation.

2. Do you weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs)?

Standard adult blood donation collects roughly 350ml - 450ml of blood. A minimum body weight of 50kg ensures safety against temporary lightheadedness.

3. Have you donated blood in the last 90 days?

Both red blood cells and iron storage levels require safe replenishment time. A 90-day cooldown period is enforced for all donors.

4. Are you currently feeling well and healthy?

You must not have any active symptoms of fever, persistent cough, cold, flu, sore throat, or general weakness today.

5. Have you had a tattoo or body piercing in the last 6 months?

To mitigate risks associated with bloodborne infections, temporary deferral is required for 6 months after receiving needle art or body piercings.

6. Are you currently pregnant or breastfeeding?

Temporary deferrals apply during pregnancy and subsequent lactation periods to avoid strain on the mother's cardiovascular health.

💡 Fast Facts
  • O Negative (O-) is the universal donor group. Their blood can be administered to any patient regardless of type.
